it hurts (so long Alice)

by Archy Will He

If the confession wasn’t bad enough, here is a little poem I wrote.

It hurts.

It hurts because I know I will never meet someone like you again.

It hurts because you are the woman I would love to grow old with.

It hurts because you flirted with me once while you were really drunk.

It hurts because I know you care about me.

It hurts because I know I won’t want to meet you again, despite how much you adore me.

It hurts because I have met death many times and I want to make things right this life, and I want to be the one that makes things right in your life.

It hurts because I want to take acid with you again, but not as friends. And discover meaning in life together. And then have crazy good fuckings until we are dead. Or until we get sick of each other.

It hurts because I know things will not be so interesting and surreal with the next girl (or guy that smells nice), unless I get extremely lucky, which is statistically improbable and it will be in a different setting and I am growing old and it will be new memories and it will not be you.

It hurts because I believe in synchronizität and I believe there is a reason I met you.

It hurts because I really appreciate the time we spent together and a part of me would love us to stay friends.

It hurts because perhaps the reason I met you is to feel this beautiful feeling of hurt and grow out of it. So I will be wiser. So I can become a psychopath, or an artist, and make a million dollar in the remaining time of my life.

It hurts because I really like you as a friend and I believe the connection we have made is something special that will grow into something really beautiful.

It hurts because I have to cut this connection right now so that I can temporarily forget about you and focus on work, instead of getting distracted because you are a fucking enchantress. And I value my work.

It hurts because I know I won’t want to meet you again, despite how much I enjoyed the sophisticated side of you. At least not for the remaining time I’m in the city.

It hurts because the clumsy side of you is super cute too, though I have fallen for the sophistication and the sadness in your eyes.

It hurts because I may never meet you again. And I will never get to discover more about this sophisticated side of you that I really love, which can be a beautiful thing too for you would stay as a mystery for the rest of my life.

It hurts because the men who you slept with that I have met were much less sophisticated than you.

It hurts because I didn’t want to be a cockblock and went drinking alone Wednesday night so you could have sex with this dude with a THC vaporizer that has been hitting on you because I wanted you to have a great night with him but it turned out to be another night that you regretted having. What a bummer. Perhaps I could have disarmed him and gone back with you to have a little joint before you sleep. A part of me did enjoy disarming guys trying to sleep with you because the social dynamics involved was fascinating. But it was tiring too and I didn’t want to cockblock you too much.

It hurts because everything has played out such beautifully in a slightly depressing but wonderful way.

It hurts because I knew I needed this rejection from you so I could move on with my life, and maybe see you again somewhere in the future when I am less prone to developing too much emotional attachment to humans that I spend more than an average amount of time with.

It hurts but it was good, wasn’t it?
